Buyer Brief

The best Michigan City acquisitions have a believable path today and a believable path tomorrow.

That usually means buyers need to test whether the location, use, and capital plan can support both current performance and future repositioning. Deals weaken fast when pricing leans on broad redevelopment optimism without enough support from the actual site, corridor, or current use.

What tends to work

  • Properties with genuine current utility and optionality
  • Sites supported by real access and neighborhood logic
  • Assets where entitlement or repositioning risk is understood early
  • Pricing that reflects execution, not just narrative

Common mistakes

  • Paying future value for weak current real estate
  • Ignoring timing risk tied to redevelopment assumptions
  • Overlooking capital needs in older assets
  • Assuming every story-driven site deserves premium pricing

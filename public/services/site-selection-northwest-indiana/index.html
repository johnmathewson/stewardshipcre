Why It Matters

Location decisions echo through operations, staffing, customers, and future growth.

Northwest Indiana offers multiple strong corridors, but they do not solve the same problem. Site selection should narrow choices based on business reality, not just map-level convenience.

FAQ

Site-selection questions

What does site selection include?

Narrowing target markets, comparing locations, evaluating access and visibility, reviewing occupancy costs, and aligning choice with operations.

Why is site selection important in Northwest Indiana?

Because different corridors serve different customer patterns, labor pools, traffic behavior, and use-case advantages.

Who uses site-selection services?

Retailers, office users, industrial occupiers, developers, and owner-users all use site-selection support when location is a meaningful business decision.

What should be compared besides rent?

Visibility, access, parking, truck flow, zoning fit, demographics, trade-area relevance, buildout cost, and operational flexibility.
